The ocean has captivated humanity for millennia, serving as both a source of sustenance and a vast frontier of exploration. With depths that remain largely mysterious, the sea covers over seventy percent of Earth's surface and contains more species than scientists have yet discovered. From bioluminescent creatures dwelling in the abyss to majestic whales traversing thousands of miles, marine life exhibits an extraordinary diversity that rivals any terrestrial ecosystem. Yet this precious realm faces unprecedented challenges from pollution, climate change, and overfishing, making ocean conservation one of the most critical environmental imperatives of our time.
